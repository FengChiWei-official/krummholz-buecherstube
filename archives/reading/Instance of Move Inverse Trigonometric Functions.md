## Text

What is `arcsin`  $x^{\prime} = g(y) = \sin^{(-1)}(y), x^{\prime} \in (-\frac{\pi}{2}, 0]$

Considering `sin`  $y = f(x) = \sin x, x \in (\frac{\pi}{2}, \pi]$

then $x^{\prime} = x - \pi$
$y = f(x-2\pi) = f((x-\pi)-\pi)$
$y = f(x^{\prime} - \pi) = -f(x^{\prime})$

$f(x) = -f(g(y))$
$f^{(-1)}(-f(x)) = g(y)$
$x = -g(y)$
