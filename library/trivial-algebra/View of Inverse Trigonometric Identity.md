## Distinctions
$$sin^{(-1)}(x) + cos^{(-1)}(x) = \frac{\pi}{2}$$
How to prove?
considering $\frac{d y}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}(sin^{(-1)}(x) + cos^{(-1)}(x) )$
we found that, 
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} = 0$
so, $y= C$. Obviously, $C = \frac{\pi}{2}$.
